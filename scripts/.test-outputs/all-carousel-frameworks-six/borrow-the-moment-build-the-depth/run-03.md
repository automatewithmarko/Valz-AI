Here's the angle: that viral story is already doing the emotional heavy lifting, so the carousel's job is to take your audience from "wow, good for her" to "wait, that's exactly what I've been feeling."

---

**Slide 1 - The Moment**

Why did that director walking away from her title feel like more than a career story this week?

Everyone's sharing it. And most of them aren't sharing it because they admire the business plan.

---

**Slide 2 - The Surface**

On the surface, it looks like courage. A bold decision, clean timing, a postcard backdrop.

It's easy to see it as someone who finally had enough and jumped.

---

**Slide 3 - The Deeper Layer**

What stayed with me was the quiet relief underneath the announcement.

Behind that post was probably a woman who had been capable for years and had long since stopped being challenged by it. Someone who had earned her seat at the table and then realised the table wasn't the goal anymore.

That's the part that's landing for so many women right now.

---

**Slide 4 - The Mirror**

When something like that hits hard, it usually means there's a version of it living in you too.

Sometimes it shines a light on how long you've been tolerating a role that looks impressive from the outside. Sometimes it surfaces the quiet realisation that progression and ownership are two very different things.

---

**Slide 5 - The Shareable Truth**

Watching someone choose work that feels like theirs can be confronting when you know you've been craving the same thing.

Awareness of that gap tends to arrive well before the decision does. You don't find yourself sharing that story for no reason.

---

**Slide 6 - The Encouragement**

If this one stirred something in you, sit with that rather than scrolling past it.

You don't need to have a plan yet. You don't need to know what the coastline looks like or what you'd even call the thing you'd build.

Start with honesty about what you actually want work to feel like. That question is usually the real beginning.
