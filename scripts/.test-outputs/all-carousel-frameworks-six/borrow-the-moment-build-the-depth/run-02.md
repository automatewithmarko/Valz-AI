Here's the angle: that viral story is already doing the emotional work for you. The carousel's job is to translate the feeling underneath it into something your audience can't quite articulate on their own, but desperately wants to.

---

**Slide 1 - The Moment**

Why did that director walking away from her title to build something from the coast feel like more than just a career pivot?

Everyone's sharing it. And it's worth asking why.

---

**Slide 2 - The Surface**

On the surface, it looks like courage. A clean decision. Someone who knew exactly what she wanted and simply went for it.

---

**Slide 3 - The Deeper Layer**

What stayed with me was the way she described her last day. There was no dramatic fallout. Just a quiet, steady certainty she'd been sitting with for a long time.

Behind that announcement was probably years of being exceptional at something that no longer felt like hers. Years of delivering for a vision that wasn't the one she'd carry home with her.

---

**Slide 4 - The Mirror**

When something like that hits hard, it usually means something closer to home is asking for attention.

Sometimes it highlights where we've been tolerating a life that fits on paper but feels tight around the edges. Sometimes it shines a light on how long we've known, and how long we've waited.

---

**Slide 5 - The Shareable Truth**

Watching someone choose ownership can be confronting when you've already outgrown the role but haven't said it out loud yet.

Awareness always arrives before the move does. You don't keep sharing someone else's story for no reason.

---

**Slide 6 - The Encouragement**

If that story stirred something in you, sit with it a moment longer before you brush it off.

There's a difference between admiring someone's boldness and recognising something in yourself. Recognition is usually your clarity asking to be taken seriously.

You don't need to hand in your notice tomorrow. But you do owe yourself an honest conversation about what you're actually building toward.
