Here's the angle: the guilt of "choosing yourself" for an hour is something first-time mums feel but rarely say out loud, so this carousel names that experience with enough specificity that the right woman feels seen immediately.

---

**Slide 1 - Identity + Tension**

I'm a first-time mum who genuinely loves her baby more than anything, and I still feel guilty for wanting one hour to myself.

---

**Slide 2 - Real Moment**

I'd be feeding at 2am, exhausted in a way I didn't know was possible, quietly Googling "postnatal Pilates" on my phone. Then put it down and tell myself I should just rest instead.

By the time I had space in the calendar, the guilt had already talked me out of it.

---

**Slide 3 - Expand the Experience**

There are days when I'd get to class and spend the first ten minutes mentally running through everything I'd left behind. Whether she was okay. Whether I should have stayed. Whether one hour was too much to ask for.

I could be grateful for the baby and still feel something quietly disappearing inside my own body.

---

**Slide 4 - Name the Invisible Problem**

The part that's rarely spoken about is how quickly a new mum stops being a person in her own right. The recalibration is constant. Every need you have gets weighed against hers, and yours almost always loses.

Moving your body stops feeling like self-care and starts feeling like something you have to justify.

---

**Slide 5 - Cultural Expectation**

There's this unspoken expectation that good mums pour everything into the baby first, and whatever's left over goes to recovery. Wanting something for yourself before the baby is "settled" feels almost selfish.

We're somehow meant to be fully present and quietly disappearing at the same time.

---

**Slide 6 - Encouragement**

You're not a bad mum for wanting to feel like yourself again. You're navigating one of the biggest identity shifts a woman goes through, and your body is still yours.

One hour of movement makes you more present, more steady. That hour is part of the care, too.

---

**Slide 7 - Invitation**

If you're a first-time mum who's been waiting until it "feels less selfish" to move your body again, you're in the right place.

This is postnatal Pilates for women who need permission to show up for themselves without the guilt.
