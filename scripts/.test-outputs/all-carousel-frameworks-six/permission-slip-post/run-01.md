Here's the angle: first-time mums carry a very specific guilt about prioritising themselves, and naming that tension precisely is what makes this one land.

---

**Slide 1 - Identity + Tension**

I'm a postnatal Pilates instructor, and I've sat with more first-time mums than I can count who whispered "I feel bad leaving him" before they even took their shoes off.

---

**Slide 2 - Real Moment**

By the time the class starts, you've already negotiated with yourself three times about whether you're allowed to be here.

Later, you'll check your phone twice before the warm-up is finished.

---

**Slide 3 - Expand the Experience**

There are days when moving your body feels like the most selfish thing on the list. You can know, logically, that you need this. And still feel a pull toward the door the entire hour.

Sometimes gratitude and exhaustion sit in the same chest. Both completely real.

---

**Slide 4 - Name the Invisible Problem**

The part that rarely gets named is the internal negotiation. The constant recalculating of what you're allowed to want for yourself now that someone else needs you so completely.

That negotiation is the heavy bit. The Pilates itself is the easy part.

---

**Slide 5 - Cultural Expectation**

Somehow we're meant to be devoted and whole at the same time, selfless but not depleted, present for everyone else but quietly fine on the inside.

One hour apparently threatens all of that.

---

**Slide 6 - Encouragement**

You're not a bad mum for needing this. You're a woman navigating one of the biggest identity shifts of your life, and your body is still asking to be part of it.

That's not guilt-worthy. That's just honest.

---

**Slide 7 - Invitation**

If you're a first-time mum who loves her baby completely and still needs an hour that belongs to you, you're in the right place.

This is where we work on that, without apology.
