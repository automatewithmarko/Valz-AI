Here's the angle: your audience is already swimming in wellness content, so the hook disrupts that loop from the inside.

---

**Slide 1 - The Curiosity Hook**

Consuming more wellness content when you're burnt out might actually be making things worse.

---

**Slide 2 - Expand the Personal Context**

I remember sitting with a full notes app, three half-read books on my bedside table, and a screen time report that said I'd spent two hours on health content that day. I'd just finished a breathwork video and still felt like I was failing at recovering. From the outside it looked like I was doing everything right.

---

**Slide 3 - Introduce the Concept**

What I didn't understand then was that the nervous system reads relentless self-optimisation as another form of stress. There's actually research around how the brain in a burnout state processes information seeking as urgency, which keeps the body in a low-level threat response. I later learned that rest requires permission, and permission requires you to stop treating your recovery like a project to manage.

---

**Slide 4 - Reframe the Narrative**

It wasn't that I lacked discipline. I was actually using productivity as a coping mechanism for the discomfort of slowing down. The wellness content gave me something to do with the anxiety of not doing enough. That felt safer than sitting in stillness.

---

**Slide 5 - Validation**

That's why you can spend an entire Sunday doing "restorative" things and still feel unsettled by evening. That's why you can tick off every habit and still feel like your body is waiting for something to go wrong. The problem often sits underneath the routine.

---

**Slide 6 - Empowered Close**

The shift for me started when I stopped measuring rest by what I consumed and started measuring it by how my body actually felt. You don't need another protocol. You might just need to trust that doing less is doing something.

---

**Slide 7 - Soft CTA (Optional)**

Save this for the next time you reach for your phone because stillness feels too uncomfortable. Send it to the woman who keeps saying she just needs to find the right routine.
