**Slide 1 - The Curiosity Hook**

Consuming more wellness content while feeling worse is actually a sign of burnout, not a lack of effort.

**Slide 2 - Expand the Personal Context**

I remember sitting with a client who had seventeen browser tabs open. Nervous system regulation, adrenal fatigue protocols, cortisol-balancing meal plans. She was exhausted just from the research. From the outside it looked like dedication. What I was actually seeing was a woman whose body was begging her to stop optimising and start resting.

---

**Slide 3 - Introduce the Concept**

What I didn't understand early in my practice was that the brain under chronic stress actively seeks control. Information feels like control. So when your body is depleted, your nervous system reaches for more data as a coping mechanism. The research backs this up, but honestly, I see it every week.

---

**Slide 4 - Reframe the Narrative**

The exhaustion underneath had nothing to do with discipline. It was the weight of treating your body like a problem to be solved instead of a system that had finally run out of runway.

---

**Slide 5 - Validation**

That's why you can spend a Sunday reading everything about rest and still wake up Monday feeling behind. That's why knowing all the right things hasn't made the tired go away.

---

**Slide 6 - Empowered Close**

The shift for most of my clients starts when they stop adding and start removing. Fewer protocols. Less research. More permission to let the body do what it already knows how to do.
