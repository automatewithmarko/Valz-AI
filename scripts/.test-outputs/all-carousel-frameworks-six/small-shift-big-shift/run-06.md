I used to believe the most productive thing I could do in the morning was get ahead of my inbox before anyone else woke up.

**Slide 2 - Reveal the Hidden Issue**

What I didn't see at the time was that I was starting every day in reactive mode. Someone else's urgency was setting the tone before I'd even had a coffee.

**Slide 3 - Expand the Pattern**

It looked like discipline. Up early, phone in hand, already moving. Behind the scenes, I was borrowing against my focus before the day had properly started. By 10am I was already scattered.

**Slide 4 - The Replacement**

The shift happened when I started leaving my phone in another room until 9am. No alarm check, no quick scroll, no "just one email." The first hour of the day became mine again.

**Slide 5 - Integration**

Since then, the morning feels different. My thinking is clearer before noon. I'm making decisions from a steadier place instead of from whatever noise arrived overnight.

**Slide 6 - Emotional Result**

It feels like I'm running the day rather than chasing it.

**Slide 7 - Invitation**
