**3 Brand Archetypes**

It gives your audience the immediate sense that someone has already done the thinking they have been avoiding. The shadow is the temptation to over-map for people who are not yet ready; you navigate it by waiting for the invitation before you offer the full system.  

The Guide. You walk slightly ahead, turning back only when someone signals they are ready to see what you see. It gives your audience permission to move at their own pace rather than being dragged. The shadow is over-responsibility; you keep it in check by remembering that your splenic authority only lights up when the right person is actually in front of you.  

The Witness. You name what others feel but have not yet said out loud. It creates the relief of being seen without having to perform. The productive tension between these three is that the Strategist wants to build the map, the Guide wants to walk it with the right person, and the Witness wants to honour what the body already knows; none of them push.
